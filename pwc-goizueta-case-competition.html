Competing in My First Business Case Competition

Placing second overall in the Goizueta Business School and PwC case competition with an all-freshman team.

I'm excited to share that I recently had the opportunity to compete in my first case competition hosted by Goizueta Business School and PwC.

With 35+ other teams competing, we were among the top 3 selected to pitch our project to PwC professionals Steven Huang, Christian Virgil, and Willi Freire, and other Goizueta business leaders. After presenting, we placed 2nd overall — and we were the only all-freshman team to present.

This was my first step into the world of business, and it wouldn't have been a success without my close friends Arjun Rajan, Aryan Patel, and David Zhang.

The challenge was to design a concept for a NYC-based coffee cart that could both maximize reach and revenue.

  • Arjun kick started our branding for "Rise & Grind Coffee" and identified local competitors.
  • David designed a dynamic menu and app UI to differentiate our brand.
  • Aryan developed financial models and sourced pedestrian traffic data.
  • I designed our slide deck and chose the location, defined our buyer profile, and developed marketing strategies given my experience in NYC.

We ultimately placed our coffee cart near "Hospital Row" and the UN corridor, targeting professionals who depend on coffee to fuel their day. The idea was inspired from my time working at NYU Langone Health, and it was exciting to see our team transform that insight into a full brand concept. This competition reinforced how working alongside people with diverse skill sets is essential to idea creation and differentiating a product.

A special thanks to Yashonandan Kakrania and Deesha Panchal, as well as Meher Jain, for organizing the tournament and supporting our group with invaluable advice. And, thank you to PwC for the opportunity to learn and make connections with Emory alumni.
